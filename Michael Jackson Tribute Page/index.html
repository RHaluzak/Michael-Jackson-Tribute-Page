The Career of Michael Jackson

Michael Jackson was a famous singer, dancer, and recording artist in America. His full legal name at birth was "Michael Joseph Jackson". The genre of music that he peformed was pop and he rose to fame in the 70s through the 90s and ultimately earned his title as the "King of Pop". He has performed many famous songs such as "Billie Jean", "Thriller" and "Beat It."

Early Life and Early Career

Michael Jackson was born in 1958 in Gary, Indiana. He was the 8th of ten total children in the family. His career began at the very young of age 6 with family his family band the "Jackson 5", led by his father, Joe Jackson. The group toured several bars and clubs performing many of their Motown Hits. They were able to sucessfully land a record deal in 1968 and signed with Motown records. Three of the most iconic hit singles performed by the Jackson 5 are: "I want you back", "ABC", and "The Lover You Save."

Solo Career

Towards the end of the 1970s, Michael was wanting to pursue a solo career. Thanks to the help of his producer, Quincy Jones, he was able to produce one of his most iconic album: "Off the Wall." The album sold over 20 million copies. He was able to attain the highest royalty rate in the music industry: 37% of all album profits. The next album he launched, "Thriller", became the number one selling album for 37 weeks with 110 million global sales and 29 million sales in the United States. This album included hits such as "Beat It" and "Billie Jean". Other albums he released are: "Bad" in 1987, "Dangerous" in 1991, with his final album, "Invincible" in 2001. In 2009, Michael Jackson died at a mansion he was renting in the district of Los Angeles.
